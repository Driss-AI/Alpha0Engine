# Backtest Report — 2026-05-30 (first real run)

**Method:** 39 hand-labeled historical cases (`scripts/seed_known_cases.py`) joined
with **real yfinance daily prices** around each snapshot date. Forward returns at
90 days. Winners = known re-raters; false positives = AI/biotech pumps that faded.

> First validation pass. Small N per lane; treat as directional, not definitive.
> Re-run on prod with the full accumulated `DailyPrice` history + live snapshots.

## L1 — AI Infrastructure ✅ VALIDATED

| Cohort | n | mean 90d | median 90d | hit (≥20%) |
|--------|--:|---------:|-----------:|-----------:|
| winners         | 11 | +45.9% | +66.1% | 64% |
| false positives |  8 | −16.5% | −22.3% | 12% |

**corr(composite score, 90d return) = +0.553.** Winners and false positives
separate cleanly and the engine's score is genuinely predictive. This lane is
cleared to produce `SETUP_READY` alerts.

## L2 — Biotech Catalysts ⚠️ NOT YET CALIBRATED

| Cohort | n | mean 90d | median 90d | hit (≥20%) |
|--------|--:|---------:|-----------:|-----------:|
| winners         | 7 | +134.9% | +20.7% | 57% |
| false positives | 7 | +61.7%  | +3.0%  | 14% |

**corr(composite score, 90d return) = +0.072 — essentially no predictive power.**
- Returns are binary/outlier-driven (mean ≫ median): a couple of names explode,
  most stall or die. A linear composite doesn't capture this.
- **5 false-positive tickers delisted** (WISA, VERB, ATHA, CYCC, INPX) — their
  ~−100% outcomes drop out of the survivors-only stats above, so the real FP
  picture is materially worse than the table shows (survivorship bias).

**Decision:** L2 is gated to `DEEP_DIVE` maximum (no `SETUP_READY`) until it has a
forward-tested calibration on live snapshots. The median separation (winner +20.7%
vs FP +3.0%) is encouraging, but the score must *rank* candidates before biotech
alerts are actionable.

## Takeaways
1. The megatrend→bottleneck thesis works where the move is demand-driven and
   gradual (AI infra) — score predicts return.
2. It does NOT yet work where the move is a single binary event (biotech) — needs
   event-conditioned scoring, not a linear composite.
3. Survivorship + delisting must be handled explicitly in the prod backtest
   (a delisted FP is the *strongest* possible "do not touch", not a missing row).
